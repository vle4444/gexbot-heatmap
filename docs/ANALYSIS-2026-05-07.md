## Why the prior AUTO ★ had limited edge

Replay of the AUTO ★ rejection logic showed the *condition* `approach
+ near wall + recent Pulse` was being met **1801 times in 4 hours**
(cooldown filters most of these to ~6 actual fires). But many of the
6 fires were drift-near-wall (`vel ≈ −0.18 SPX/5s`) not actual
approaches. Examples from the data:

```
{"ci":3257,"t":"15:14:08","type":"rejection","wall":7375,"spot":"7376.34","tier":3,"vel":"-0.18"}
{"ci":3258,"t":"15:14:09","type":"rejection","wall":7375,"spot":"7376.25","tier":3,"vel":"-0.20"}
{"ci":3259,"t":"15:14:10","type":"rejection","wall":7375,"spot":"7376.10","tier":3,"vel":"-0.27"}
```

These tick-level moves don't represent a real approach to the wall —
they're just market microstructure noise.

## Fix — decel + min-approach filters (v0.8.10)

Two new sensitivity parameters:

- `minApproachVel` (SPX points per 5-snap window) — the prior 5s
  velocity must exceed this magnitude. **Filters drift.**
- `decelRatio` — current velocity must be `< |prior| × decelRatio`.
  **The "wall is starting to hold" signature.**

Replay on the same session, `low` sensitivity (decelRatio=0.55,
minApproachVel=2.0):

```
4 fires / 4h. The standout fire is at 15:34 (wall=7375 v_prev=+7.75,
v_now=-2.74) — captures the day's biggest pop-and-reverse setup
(7385 high → 7358 low, 21.7 pt) as a single annotation that lines
up with both the high (lead=-5s) and the low (lead=+22s).
```

Replay on `med` sensitivity (decelRatio=0.7, minApproachVel=1.0):

```
6 fires / 4h. Includes the 17:27 low-of-day bounce (wall=7320,
v_prev=-1.83, v_now=-1.17, lead=10s) and the 17:53 LOD re-test.
```

The breakout branch gets a symmetric `accelRatio = 1/decelRatio`
filter — only fires when spot is moving *faster* through the wall
than into it.

## Other detectors prototyped — not implemented

- **`accel`** (per-strike d/dt of |dir|): same firing pattern as the
  v0.8.9 Surprise detector. No lead-time advantage. Skipped.

- **`wall_flow`** (cumulative |dir| over a rolling window at major
  walls): flow *accumulates after* bounces as activity pivots to the
  wall that just held. Useful as a confirmation visualization but
  not predictive. Skipped — could be revisited as a pure render-
  layer feature.

## Where the predictive edge actually lives

Three things showed real lead time on the bounces:

1. **Wall stickiness** — every headline bounce was at a wall with
   substantial holdSnaps (e.g. M+ 7335 was sticky through the entire
   17:00–18:00 hour). AUTO ★'s ★★★ tier already filters for this.

2. **Spot-velocity *deceleration*** — the bounce signature is "fast
   approach, slowing into the level". This is what v0.8.10 adds.

3. **Multi-detector confluence** — when 2+ of `surprise` / `consensus`
   / `rank_shift` fire on the same strike within seconds, the lift
   is genuine. Not yet wired into AUTO ★ as a quality-boost; flagged
   for follow-up.
